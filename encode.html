PHASE 01 · ENCODE

What we capture during our intake process becomes the operating brain that dials in your self-driving departments.

// WHAT THIS IS

The Encode is a structured interview designed to capture the things we can't learn from your website — your voice, your authority, your anti-patterns.

▸ The stuff only you know._
// HOW THIS WORKS
01You speak. The engine never invents your brand.
02The engine learns. What you say becomes how it ships.
03You stay the operator. Approve, edit, or kill — every week.

WHAT WE COVER

01
GOALS
What "winning" looks like for this engine — in your own words.
Six months in, what result makes this engine worth every dollar?
Is it awareness, conversion, retail credibility, investor signal, or something else?
SAMPLE
"Make our content less boring, more trend-forward. Drive trial of Original + Kids. Position Willa's as the cleanest plant milk in a crowded category."
02
FOUNDATION
Why you exist. In one sentence you'd stake the company on.
If your toughest customer described why you exist, what would they say?
What's the line you'd put on the tombstone of the brand?
SAMPLE
"Real food, passed down. Reinvented forward. We're the first whole-plant oat milk — the only one using the entire oat groat, not processed syrup."
03
AUTHORITY
The claim only you get to make — and the proof behind it.
What can ONLY you credibly say that no competitor can?
For each claim on your stack — what's the third-party proof?
SAMPLE
"Only Willa's: WholePlant™ — the entire oat groat, not syrup. Proof: 1g sugar, 4g+ protein, Yuka 94/100 (Original) and 100/100 (Kids), Good Food Award (Chocolate)."
04
AUDIENCE
Who you're for — and who you're not.
Who picks you up off the shelf? Who do you not want buying you?
What question are they asking right before they reach for you?
SAMPLE
"Conscious mom reading the back label. GLP-1 user prioritizing nutrient density. Not the price-shopper at mass — they have other options. The household where ingredients matter."
05
VOICE
How you actually sound — by example, not adjective.
Paste 5 lines — from anywhere — that sound exactly like you.
Paste 3 that don't. Tell us why they miss.
SAMPLE
"'Designed for kids' tastebuds. And shhh…. it's even parent approved.' Warm + wink + assertive in three letters. Grandmother's kitchen with a strong POV."
06
COMPETITORS
Stance, not just a list. Who you are — and aren't — compared to whom.
Named list — internal only. For each: friend, enemy, adjacent, or refuse-to-name?
What's the approved comparison framing when we need to show difference?
SAMPLE
"Oatly: the incumbent we displace (12 ingredients, oat syrup). Elmhurst: closest clean play, but not organic. Never name them publicly — only 'vs. Average Category.'"
07
CREATORS
Who's already in your orbit — and who actually fits the story.
Current relationships — ambassadors, press, creators. Names.
What profile of creator fits your story? Who doesn't, even if they'd say yes?
SAMPLE
"Christina (founder) is the primary voice. Dietitians, pediatricians, mom creators for Kids. Not generic wellness influencers — wrong story. Gifting model, sometimes paid."
08
GUARDRAILS
What can't ship. What you've tried and hated. What you refuse.
What would sound great in a caption but can never go public — and why?
Content you've shipped and regretted? Formats you'd refuse even if they'd go viral?
SAMPLE
"Internal only: growth metrics, retail door counts, 'come for the label, stay for the taste' (investor line). Refuse: lattes >1x per 2 weeks, pasta roundups, recipes where Willa's is just a trace ingredient."
